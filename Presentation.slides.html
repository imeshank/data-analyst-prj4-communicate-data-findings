Ford GoBike System Data Exploration¶

by Imesha Kuruppu¶

Investigation Overview¶

In this investigation, I wanted to analyze user behavior affecting the use of the GoBike system. The investigation is done to find in which situations bike usage is high and how average trip duration varies with the user characteristics. The main focus is given to find characteristics(user type, age, gender) of users and analyze how average trip duration and demand for bikes varies with those characteristics.

Dataset Overview¶

Initially, this data-set included 183412 rows and 16 columns. This dataset has bike-sharing data of February month in 2019. After the preliminary data-wrangling, this data set included data for 174952 bike trips along with 16 columns. For each bike trip, it included information about the trip duration, start/end time, start/end station id and name, bike id, user type, member birth year, and member gender. 'start_date', 'start_day', 'start_hour', 'duration_min', and 'user_age' columns derived from existing columns.

Average trip duration by age vs gender vs user type¶

When we consider subscribers,
The average trip duration is high among younger(20-35) and older people(50-65).
Av. trip duration of other gender people(16 min) is higher, then females and males have lowest av. trip duration(around 10 mins). Older females(50-65) use bikes for long trips(13 mins)

When we consider customers,
Average trip duration is highest among young(20-35) other gender people(29 min)
Older(50-65) females also have a long trip duration(26 min)
Males have the lowest average trip duration (20 min)

Conclusion¶

When we consider the bike usage trend of users we can conclude,

  • Subscribers use the bike-sharing system more than customers.
  • Males use the bike-sharing system most.
  • There is a high demand for bikes during 7-9 am and 4-6 pm during weekdays from all users. This implies both users use bikes for commute purposes.
  • Customers have a high demand for bikes during the weekend daytime.
  • Bike usage of users over 50 years is less when compared to other users.

Conclusion¶

When we consider the average trip duration of users we can conclude,

  • Customers' average trip duration is always higher than the subscribers' average trip duration.
  • During the weekends both users' average trip duration increases when compared to weekdays.
  • Average trip duration is higher among young(20-35 age) and old people(50-65 age).
  • When we consider the gender, Other gender people use bikes for longer trips. Males' average trip duration is always less when compared with other genders.

It is interesting to find that, the users who use the bike-sharing system more frequently use it for shorter trips and users who use the bike-sharing system less frequently use it for longer trips.
